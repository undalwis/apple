  • Frequently Asked Questions

    • Why do you want to work for Apple?
    • I want to work at Apple for two reasons.

      (1) I want to create an impact within University Relations and Inclusion & Diversity. I believe knowledge is power, but power is useless when you can't share the opportunity it brings with those around you. So, every experience of mine focuses on aiding the people, companies, and institutions of tomorrow. For me, Apple is the hallmark of what tomorrow brings and University students from various backgrounds are what tomorrow holds. With the opportunity to work as a G&A Intern, I will highlight our strengths as partners rather than our weaknesses as individuals, bringing the best and most diverse talent to Apple.

      (2) I want to use data to improve Apple's University Relations processes. At age 10, in an effort to raise enough money for a class trip, I asked my dad if I could start a business to sell my art online. My dad said no. So, I took it upon myself to write a detailed business recommendation with data points on which products I would sell, how much I would earn, how long it would take, and why the trade-offs were worth the investment. It was this process that first pushed me into the world of analyzing data to uncover areas of opportunity. From what I know, Apple is a very data-driven company and as a G&A Intern, I will use data as a cornerstrone to the solutions I bring.
    • Why did you create the 'Apple x Umeesha' website?
    • Apple's success comes from their customer-centric focus and their willingness to experiment, fail, and learn. So, I created the 'Apple x Umeesha' website to pay it forward for all the work Apple does for us. I created an application that would be Apple recruiter-centric to showcase my willingness to also experiment, fail, and learn. At the end of the day, Apple deserves a G&A Intern who will exceed expectations for the company.
    • What interests you about University Relations and Inclusion & Diversity?
    • At age 6, my teacher advised my parents to enroll me in Special Education. I couldn't communicate via written or oral means. So, the idea of attending University seemed foreign. But in a whirlwind of events, I pushed through the learning disability and landed at one of the best Universities that California had to offer. However, I believe I am not the only student capable of defying the odds. I believe in the strength and potential of every individual, and had I not had someone like that to believe in me, I would not have the career I have today. For that reason, I dream of working in University Relations to give students the opportunity to be the best of the best while also working with rejected students to help them learn from their experiences of applying to Apple.

      Also, as a kid, I tried shortening my ethnic name to something more 'American-sounding.' An adult had mistakenly advised me that my ethnic name would cause recruiters to push my application to the back of the line. As an immigrant, first-generation student, and woman of color, I want to empower individuals who wrongly believe that their background will negatively impact their ability to succeed. At Apple, it's the diversity of its people that help push its innovative ideas into every industry, and as an Inclusion & Diversity intern, I will empower individuals from various backgrounds to leave the world better than we found it.
